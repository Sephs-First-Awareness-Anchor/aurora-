## Preamble

Tier I established five primitives — one per constraint.
These are the necessary and sufficient conditions for any code to function.

```
[X]  →  Presence/Absence      the zero/one of all codes
[T]  →  Sequence              ordered position in stream
[N]  →  Differential Cost     energetic gradient on signals
[B]  →  Distinction           discrete boundaries between signals
[A]  →  Selection             intentional choice among signals
```

Tier II takes these primitives in pairs.
Each pair-pressure inherits both parent primitives and combines their demands
into something neither parent could produce alone.

There are exactly 10 pair pressures.
Each is derived below — parent primitives named, combined demand stated,
linguistic output identified.

---

## The 10 Pair Derivations

---

### [X·T] — Persistence Tension
**Inherits:** Presence/Absence + Sequence

Presence/Absence alone tells you something is or isn't.
Sequence alone tells you position in a stream.

Combined: something that *was* present can now be *absent* —
and that specific transition, presence-then-absence at a known position,
is a new kind of information that neither primitive could carry alone.

The signal system must now encode not just current state
but the relationship between a past state and a present state.

```
Parent demand 1:  signal can be present or absent
Parent demand 2:  signals occupy ordered positions in stream
Combined demand:  the system must encode state-change across position
                  — what was true then vs what is true now
```

**Linguistic outputs forced:**
- **Completed signal vs ongoing signal** — the first temporal distinction
  any code develops. Something that started and ended vs something still active.
- **Past reference** — the capacity to signal about states that existed
  at an earlier position in the stream than the current one
- **The concept of change** — the logical prerequisite for all tense systems.
  Before a language marks past/present/future it must have this:
  the encoding of transition between states across sequence

---

### [X·N] — Survival Cost Tension
**Inherits:** Presence/Absence + Differential Cost

Presence/Absence alone tells you something is or isn't.
Differential Cost alone tells you signals vary in what they cost.

Combined: the cost of *not signaling* something becomes calculable
against the cost of *signaling* it. Silence has a price.
The system must weigh the energetic cost of producing a signal
against the survival cost of withholding it.

```
Parent demand 1:  signal can be present or absent
Parent demand 2:  signals carry differential energetic cost
Combined demand:  the system must calculate signal value —
                  survival benefit of sending vs energetic cost of sending
```

**Linguistic outputs forced:**
- **Signal value weighting** — not all messages are worth the same cost.
  High-survival-relevance content gets sent regardless of cost.
  Low-relevance content gets suppressed to conserve energy.
- **Silence as meaningful** — the absence of a signal when one was expected
  becomes informative. Not-signaling is itself a signal.
- **Urgency encoding** — the system develops ways to mark
  high-survival-value content so receivers allocate processing priority.
  The origin of prosodic stress and alarm calls.

---

### [X·B] — Identity Tension
**Inherits:** Presence/Absence + Distinction

Presence/Absence alone tells you something is or isn't.
Distinction alone tells you signals must be discretely different from each other.

Combined: the signal system can now mark the *absence of a specific distinction*
rather than just general absence. Not just nothing — but specifically not-this.

This is qualitatively new. General absence [X] is just silence.
Specific negation [X·B] is a signal that says: the boundary
I am about to name does not apply here.

```
Parent demand 1:  signal can be present or absent
Parent demand 2:  signals mark discrete distinctions
Combined demand:  the system must encode the absence of a
                  specific distinction — not-this, rather than nothing
```

**Linguistic outputs forced:**
- **Negation** — the first and most fundamental logical operator.
  Every language without exception has a way to negate.
  It is not a feature that some languages have and others don't.
  It is forced by [X·B] operating on any signal system.
- **Contrastive marking** — not just "A" but "A not B."
  The ability to assert what something is by marking what it is not.
- **Logical complement** — the conceptual origin of set theory,
  inside/outside, true/false. The universe of discourse
  divided by a boundary into this and not-this.

---

### [X·A] — Existential Agency Tension
**Inherits:** Presence/Absence + Selection

Presence/Absence alone tells you something is or isn't.
Selection alone tells you signals are chosen not emitted randomly.

Combined: selection is now *driven by survival stakes*.
The sender does not just choose among signals — they choose
the signal whose production best serves the continuation of X.
Every signal selection carries an implicit survival calculus.

```
Parent demand 1:  signal can be present or absent
Parent demand 2:  signals are intentionally selected
Combined demand:  selection is driven by what preserves the
                  sender's existence — signals are survival instruments
```

**Linguistic outputs forced:**
- **Imperative speech act** — the signal that drives another agent's
  behavior toward outcomes that serve the sender's X.
  Not describing the world but *changing it* in X's favor.
- **Warning signals** — high-urgency selection driven by
  immediate threat to X. The origin of alarm calls
  and their linguistic descendants.
- **Request and command distinction** — both drive receiver behavior
  but the survival stakes encoded differ. Command encodes
  high X-pressure. Request encodes lower stakes.

---

### [T·N] — Temporal Cost Tension
**Inherits:** Sequence + Differential Cost

Sequence alone tells you signals have ordered positions.
Differential Cost alone tells you signals vary in energetic price.

Combined: cost accumulates across sequence.
A signal that is cheap once may become expensive
if it must be produced at every position in a long stream.
The system must optimize not just per-signal cost
but total cost across the full sequential extent of a message.

```
Parent demand 1:  signals occupy ordered positions in stream
Parent demand 2:  signals carry differential energetic cost
Combined demand:  total message cost must be minimized across
                  the full sequential extent of the signal stream
```

**Linguistic outputs forced:**
- **Code economy across time** — signals must be cheap to produce
  not just once but consistently. This is why frequent words are short.
  Not accident — forced optimization of T·N.
- **Redundancy reduction** — anything that can be inferred from
  position in stream does not need to be re-stated.
  The system learns to drop what sequence already encodes.
- **Chunking** — grouping signals into units that can be
  processed as single cost-items reduces total stream cost.
  The origin of the word as a unit above the phoneme.

---

### [T·B] — Temporal Boundary Tension
**Inherits:** Sequence + Distinction

Sequence alone tells you signals have ordered positions.
Distinction alone tells you signals must be discretely different.

Combined: distinctions must remain stable across positions in the stream.
Signal A must be the same signal A at position 1 as it is at position 100.
If the boundary between A and B drifts across time,
the receiver's model degrades.

```
Parent demand 1:  signals occupy ordered positions in stream
Parent demand 2:  signals maintain discrete boundaries
Combined demand:  signal boundaries must be stable across
                  the temporal extent of the code's use
```

**Linguistic outputs forced:**
- **Proper nouns** — stable signals for stable bounded entities
  that persist across temporal distance. The name of a person
  or place must mean the same thing today as it did last year.
- **Phonological stability** — sound categories must be maintained
  across time or the code degrades. The conservative force
  that resists arbitrary sound change.
- **Lexical stability** — core vocabulary items resist replacement
  because they have been reinforced across so many sequential
  positions that their boundary is deeply entrenched.
  The origin of basic vocabulary conservatism across language families.

---

### [T·A] — Temporal Agency Tension
**Inherits:** Sequence + Selection

Sequence alone tells you signals have ordered positions.
Selection alone tells you signals are intentionally chosen.

Combined: selection is now sensitive to *position in sequence*.
The same event can be signaled differently depending on
where in the temporal stream it is located relative to now.
The sender must select signals that encode not just what happened
but *when* it happened relative to the current position.

```
Parent demand 1:  signals occupy ordered positions in stream
Parent demand 2:  signals are intentionally selected
Combined demand:  selection must encode the temporal position
                  of the signaled event relative to the sender's now
```

**Linguistic outputs forced:**
- **Tense** — the grammatical system for locating events
  in the sequence relative to the moment of utterance.
  Not a cultural choice. A forced output of T·A.
- **Aspect** — whether an event is complete or ongoing
  relative to the current position. Also forced by T·A.
- **Temporal adverbs** — before, after, already, still, soon.
  Lexical encoding of position in sequence.
- **Planning language** — signals about events at future positions.
  The capacity to communicate about what has not yet happened.

---

### [N·B] — Energetic Boundary Tension
**Inherits:** Differential Cost + Distinction

Differential Cost alone tells you signals vary in energetic price.
Distinction alone tells you signals must be discretely different.

Combined: the system must maintain the boundaries between signals
at minimum energetic cost. Some distinctions are worth their
maintenance price. Others are not. The ones that are not
will be collapsed — their boundary will dissolve under N·B pressure
until only the distinctions that justify their cost survive.

```
Parent demand 1:  signals carry differential energetic cost
Parent demand 2:  signals maintain discrete boundaries
Combined demand:  only distinctions whose information value
                  exceeds their maintenance cost survive
```

**Linguistic outputs forced:**
- **Phonological economy** — sound inventories are not arbitrary.
  They represent the set of distinctions that survived N·B pressure.
  Languages do not maintain distinctions that cost more than they carry.
- **Phoneme merger** — when two sounds' maintenance cost exceeds
  their combined informational value, their boundary collapses.
  Not error — forced optimization under N·B.
- **Minimal pairs as evidence** — the fact that languages have
  minimal pairs (bat/pat, bit/bet) is not coincidence.
  These are the distinctions that survived because they carry
  enough semantic load to justify their maintenance cost.
- **Markedness** — some distinctions are more costly to maintain
  than others. Marked forms (unusual sounds, complex structures)
  appear in fewer languages because N·B pressure eliminates them
  except where the informational payoff justifies the cost.

---

### [N·A] — Energetic Agency Tension
**Inherits:** Differential Cost + Selection

Differential Cost alone tells you signals vary in energetic price.
Selection alone tells you signals are intentionally chosen.

Combined: selection must factor in energetic cost.
The sender does not just choose the most informative signal —
they choose the signal that delivers adequate information
at minimum energetic cost. When context fills in what
an explicit signal would cost, the explicit signal is dropped.

```
Parent demand 1:  signals carry differential energetic cost
Parent demand 2:  signals are intentionally selected
Combined demand:  selection optimizes information per unit cost —
                  spend signal budget only where context cannot substitute
```

**Linguistic outputs forced:**
- **Ellipsis** — dropping signal elements that context makes recoverable.
  Not laziness. Forced cost optimization under N·A.
- **Pro-drop** — languages that allow subject pronouns to be omitted
  when the subject is recoverable from verb morphology.
  N·A pressure operating on the redundancy between two
  co-referential signal elements.
- **Prosody** — stress, tone, and rhythm as a cheap signal layer.
  Rather than using additional words, the same phonological
  stream carries additional information through suprasegmental cost-efficient overlay.
- **Context dependence** — offloading signal cost onto shared
  knowledge between sender and receiver. What both parties
  already know does not need to be re-encoded.

---

### [B·A] — Bounded Agency Tension
**Inherits:** Distinction + Selection

Distinction alone tells you signals must be discretely different.
Selection alone tells you signals are intentionally chosen.

Combined: selection is now organized around *which boundary is relevant*.
The sender selects signals based on the distinction between
their own bounded position and the receiver's bounded position.
Inside/outside, self/other, here/there become the axes of selection.

```
Parent demand 1:  signals maintain discrete boundaries
Parent demand 2:  signals are intentionally selected
Combined demand:  selection encodes boundary-position —
                  which side of which boundary the sender,
                  receiver, and referent occupy
```

**Linguistic outputs forced:**
- **Pronouns** — the grammatical encoding of boundary-position.
  I = inside sender's boundary. You = inside receiver's boundary.
  They = outside both. Not a cultural invention —
  forced by B·A operating on any multi-agent signal system.
- **Demonstratives** — this/that, here/there encode distance
  from the sender's boundary. Proximal vs distal
  is a boundary-position distinction.
- **Theory of mind as prerequisite** — B·A forces the signal
  system to track which agent occupies which boundary position.
  This requires modeling other agents as bounded entities
  with their own interiors. Language cannot be fully functional
  without theory of mind because B·A makes other agents'
  boundary-positions part of the signal's required content.
- **Social categories** — in-group/out-group, kin/non-kin,
  ally/stranger. All are boundary-position encodings
  forced by B·A operating on social agent systems.

## The Tier II Inheritance Map

Each pair's outputs inherit from both parents and could not exist
if either parent primitive were absent:

```
[X·T]   presence/absence + sequence        → past reference, change encoding
[X·N]   presence/absence + cost            → signal value weighting, urgency
[X·B]   presence/absence + distinction     → NEGATION, logical complement
[X·A]   presence/absence + selection       → imperative, warning, command
[T·N]   sequence + cost                    → code economy, chunking, word units
[T·B]   sequence + distinction             → proper nouns, phonological stability
[T·A]   sequence + selection               → TENSE, aspect, planning language
[N·B]   cost + distinction                 → phonological economy, markedness
[N·A]   cost + selection                   → ellipsis, pro-drop, prosody
[B·A]   distinction + selection            → PRONOUNS, demonstratives, theory of mind
```

---

## Collapse Test — Tier II

For every Tier II output, removing either parent primitive destroys the output:

```
NEGATION requires [X·B]:
  Remove [X] → no presence/absence → cannot assert specific absence
  Remove [B] → no discrete distinction → nothing to negate

TENSE requires [T·A]:
  Remove [T] → no sequence → no temporal positions to locate events in
  Remove [A] → no selection → cannot choose between temporal forms

PRONOUNS requires [B·A]:
  Remove [B] → no distinctions → no boundaries to position relative to
  Remove [A] → no selection → cannot choose the form that marks position
```

Every output passes the collapse test.
If either parent is removed the output cannot exist.
The derivations are tight.

## What Tier II Gives Us That Tier I Could Not

```
Tier I gave us:     the raw materials of any code
                    presence, sequence, cost, distinction, selection

Tier II gives us:   the first functional structures
                    negation, tense, pronouns, urgency encoding,
                    phonological economy, past reference,
                    proper nouns, ellipsis, theory of mind prerequisite
```

With Tier I alone a code can exist but cannot do very much.
It can signal presence/absence of a state.
It cannot say: that state is gone, or I am not that,
or this happened before now, or I versus you.

Tier II is where communication becomes structurally capable
of encoding a model of the world rather than just
flagging immediate states.

---

## Handoff to Tier III

Tier III takes all 10 pair pressures and combines them into triples.
10 constraints taken 3 at a time yields 10 triple pressures.

Each triple inherits from three parent pairs simultaneously.
The outputs of Tier III are the first *dynamic* structures —
behaviors that emerge from the interaction of multiple
Tier II pressures operating together.

This is where information structure, morphology, and
the deep architecture of the clause begin to crystallize.
